STAY SAFE ONLINE — PREVENTION CHECKLIST
Samagra Gune | 25BCE10462 | Task 5 — Cybercrime Awareness

GENERAL ONLINE SAFETY

1. Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, CVV, or password with anyone — not even someone
   claiming to be from your bank, a company, or the government.

2. Always check the sender's email address carefully before clicking any link.
   Phishing emails often use addresses like support@paytm-helpdesk.net instead
   of official domains.

3. Do not click on links sent via SMS or WhatsApp from unknown numbers, even if
   they look like they are from a bank or government portal.

4. Use a different password for each important account (email, banking, college
   portal). A password manager can help you keep track of them.

5. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your email, social media, and any
   platform that supports it. This adds a second layer of protection even if
   your password is stolen.

6. Be careful about what you post publicly. Your college name, hostel address,
   and daily routine can be used against you by scammers.

UPI AND FINANCIAL SAFETY (IMPORTANT)

7. Remember this clearly: you do NOT need to enter your UPI PIN to RECEIVE money.
   If someone asks you to enter your PIN to accept a payment, it is a scam.
   A collect/pay request takes money OUT of your account.

8. Before scanning any QR code for a payment, verify the merchant name shown
   on your UPI app before confirming. Fraudsters place fake QR codes over
   legitimate ones at shops and stalls.

DEVICE AND ACCOUNT SAFETY

9. Do not use public Wi-Fi (at cafes, airports, or malls) to log in to your bank
   or make UPI payments. Use your mobile data instead.

10. Log out of your accounts on shared or college lab computers after every session.
    Do not save passwords on browsers that others can access.

IF SOMETHING GOES WRONG

Report any cybercrime immediately at:

   National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
   Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (available across India, 24x7)

The faster you report, the higher the chance of recovering lost money or
stopping further damage. Do not wait or feel embarrassed — these crimes
happen to careful people too.
